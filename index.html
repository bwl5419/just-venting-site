The Risk Is Real

Your dryer vent could be the most dangerous thing in your home.

According to the National Fire Protection Association, dryers and washing machines cause an estimated 13,820 home structure fires every year in the United States. These fires result in deaths, hundreds of injuries, and more than $238 million in property damage annually.

The leading cause, by a significant margin, is failure to clean. Not a mechanical defect. Not an electrical fault. Just accumulated lint that nobody removed. The fire starts in the vent, travels through the wall, and by the time smoke detectors go off, the damage is already done.

Most homeowners don't think about their dryer vent until something goes wrong. A 30-minute cleaning appointment is genuinely one of the easiest, most cost-effective home safety improvements you can make. The question isn't whether it's worth doing. It's whether you've done it recently.

Common Questions

Everything you're
probably wondering.

The NFPA recommends having your dryer vent inspected and cleaned at least once per year. Households that do many loads of laundry per week, use a gas dryer, or have longer or more complex vent routes may benefit from cleaning every six months. If you've never had it cleaned and you've lived in your home for more than a year, it's already overdue.
Most appointments take between 45 minutes and 90 minutes depending on the length and routing of your vent line and how long it's been since the last cleaning. Longer duct runs or older flexible hosing can add time. Keith will give you an accurate estimate when you book so there are no surprises.
The most common warning signs are: clothes taking longer than one cycle to fully dry, the dryer or your laundry feeling unusually hot after a cycle, a burning or musty smell during operation, excessive lint around the dryer door or vent area, or it's simply been more than a year since your last cleaning. Any one of these is reason enough to schedule an inspection.
Just make sure the dryer is accessible and the laundry area has some clearance around it so Keith can work comfortably. Keith handles everything else: equipment, cleanup, all of it. There's nothing special you need to do or buy beforehand.
